Lead-Time Demand Distribution

Red shaded right tail = P(stockout). The normal loss function NL(z) is not this area — it measures average overshoot beyond R in standardized units, weighted by how far demand exceeds R.

Normal Loss Function — What It Means

NL(z) ≠ P(stockout). It measures the expected overshoot beyond R in standardized units. Three outcomes below show why shortage size differs even within the stockout tail.

D < R: no stockout, shortage = 0
D just above R: small overshoot
D far above R: large overshoot
Takeaway: red tail = probability; NL(z) = size-weighted average of those shortage gaps.

Computed Outputs

Safety stock → R position → P(stockout) → NL(z) → nR = σ·NL(z).
