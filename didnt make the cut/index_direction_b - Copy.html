Key Concepts

P(stockout)
Chance demand during lead time exceeds reorder point R. Shown by the red right tail.
NL(z)
Average standardized overshoot beyond R. Not the shaded area — it weights by how far demand exceeds R.
nR
Expected units short per cycle. Equals σX × NL(z): converting standardized overshoot to actual units.
Safety stock

What Is the Normal Loss Function?

NL(z) is not the probability of a stockout. It measures the expected size of the overshoot beyond R in standardized units. The three outcomes below illustrate why shortage size varies even within the tail.

Left of R: no stockout → shortage = 0
Just right of R: stockout, but small overshoot
Far right of R: stockout, large overshoot
Key: P(stockout) = area of the red tail. NL(z) captures the weighted average of the shortage gap across all those right-tail outcomes.

Show the Math

Step-by-step derivation
Step 1
Convert reorder point R to a z-score
z = (R − μX) / σX
Step 2
Evaluate the normal loss function
NL(z) = f(z) − z [1 − F(z)]
Step 3
Convert standardized overshoot to actual units
nR = σX · NL(z)
f(z) = (1/√2π) · exp(−z²/2)   [standard normal PDF]
F(z) = ∫−∞z f(t) dt   [standard normal CDF]
